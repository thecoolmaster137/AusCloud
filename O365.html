Frequently Asked Questions

Do you have any Office 365 queries? We're here to assist! Below you'll find answers to the most commonly asked Office 365 questions.

What is Office 365 (O365)?

Office 365 is a subscription-based cloud service offered by Microsoft, combining popular desktop applications like Word, Excel, and PowerPoint with robust collaboration tools like Teams and SharePoint, all accessible from any device.

How does O365 differ from the traditional Microsoft Office suite?

Unlike the traditional one-time purchase Office suite, O365 offers ongoing updates and enhancements, cloud-based collaboration tools, and the ability to access apps from anywhere, anytime, on any device with an internet connection.

Is my data secure with Office 365?

Yes, Microsoft takes security seriously and offers a comprehensive set of advanced security features including encryption, two-factor authentication, and threat intelligence to ensure data protection.

Can I use Office 365 offline?

While O365 is cloud-centric, apps like Word, Excel, and PowerPoint can be installed on devices for offline access. Any changes made offline will sync to the cloud once reconnected.

How does the collaboration work in O365?

Tools like Microsoft Teams, SharePoint, and OneDrive enable real-time collaboration. Users can co-author documents, hold virtual meetings, share files, and more, all within the O365 environment.

Which devices and operating systems are compatible with Office 365?

O365 is compatible with a wide range of devices, including PCs, Macs, Android devices, and iOS devices. Most major browsers are also supported for web-based access.

How often does Microsoft update features and functionalities in O365?

Microsoft frequently rolls out updates, including new features, enhancements, and security measures. Subscribers receive these updates automatically, ensuring they always have the latest tools and protections.
